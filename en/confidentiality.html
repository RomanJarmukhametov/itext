4. Use of cookies

What are cookies.

A cookie is a small text file sent to your browser from a website you visit and then stored on your computer's hard drive. If you reconnect to this site, the cookies are re-read and recognized by the site that sent them. When you visit a website, you may receive cookies from third-party sites (called "third-party cookies"), such as links to web pages on other domains.

Cookies can be used for various purposes, such as recognizing a user's device by the website, tracking sessions, and remembering specific information about users who access the server.

Types of cookies

Technical cookies are necessary for the proper functioning of the site and for you to benefit from its services.

Functionality cookies make it easier for you to navigate the site, remember your selections, and tailor the functionality to your requirements.

The site uses analytics cookies to collect information about the site's use, visited pages, and any errors that may occur while browsing. These cookies do not collect personally identifiable information about your computer. All information collected by such cookies is combined into a single array and, therefore, anonymous.

However, the user can disable analytics cookies anytime by using the instructions below.

Disabling cookies

You can allow or disallow cookies anytime and erase any installed cookies by adjusting your browser settings. The company cannot guarantee the site's proper operation if cookies are disabled.

The procedure for adjusting settings differs depending on the browser.
